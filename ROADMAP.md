# Roadmap

This roadmap organizes the project by maturity and risk. The goal is to move from safe reproduction of established communication support methods toward a more complete AI-assisted toolkit.

## v0.1 — Clickable concept prototype

Status: completed

Purpose:

- Communicate the project direction.
- Show the three core modules.
- Make the project concrete enough to discuss with mentors, professors, and organization staff.

Completed:

- Visual Support Generator mockup.
- Communication Partner Coach mockup.
- Communication Event Review Assistant mockup.
- Safety boundaries displayed in the interface.
- Static HTML prototype.

Limitations:

- Outputs are preset examples.
- No real AI generation yet.
- No user testing yet.
- No professional validation yet.

## v0.2 — Real Visual Support Generator

Reference methods:

- visual schedules;
- step-by-step visual routines;
- social stories;
- visual rules and transition supports.

Goal:

Allow a parent, teacher, or volunteer to input a real-life scenario and generate a simple visual support package.

Example input:

> Tomorrow is the child’s first day at summer camp. They may feel nervous because they will meet new teachers and classmates.

Expected output:

- 5–8 visual steps;
- simple child-facing sentences;
- adult-facing guidance;
- printable layout;
- optional “I need help / I need a break / I don’t understand” communication cards.

Why this version comes first:

- low risk;
- easy to understand;
- useful in real settings;
- does not require AI to infer the child’s intention;
- suitable for summer camp scenarios.

## v0.3 — Communication Partner Coach

Reference methods:

- Communication Partner Training;
- Aided Language Modeling;
- simplified language;
- wait time;
- confirmation of understanding.

Goal:

Help adults adjust their communication instead of placing all responsibility on autistic children.

Core feature:

Adult input:

> Hurry up and pack your things, then wash your hands. We need to leave now or we’ll be late.

AI output:

1. Pack your bag.
2. Wash your hands.
3. Then we will leave.
4. I will wait for two minutes.

Additional coaching:

- give one instruction at a time;
- reduce pressure words;
- use visual support when possible;
- allow processing time;
- do not assume silence means refusal.

Why this matters:

This module represents the “two-way communication” principle. It does not only train autistic children to adapt; it also helps neurotypical communication partners communicate more clearly and respectfully.

## v0.4 — Communication Cards and Rights Buttons

Reference methods:

- AAC;
- picture communication;
- core vocabulary boards;
- speech-generating devices.

Goal:

Generate scenario-specific communication cards that support needs, feelings, refusal, clarification, and autonomy.

Important card categories:

- I need help.
- I need a break.
- Too loud.
- I don’t understand.
- Please say it again.
- Stop.
- No.
- You misunderstood me.
- I want to choose by myself.

Why this version matters:

Many communication tools focus heavily on requesting objects. This version should also support refusal, boundaries, confusion, repair, and self-advocacy.

## v0.5 — Communication Event Review Assistant

Reference methods:

- ABC observation records;
- behavior event documentation;
- teacher observation notes;
- case review;
- early functional behavior analysis documentation principles.

Goal:

Help adults document communication difficulties without immediately labeling or diagnosing the child.

Input:

> During a transition, the teacher said they were moving to another room. The child stood still, looked at their bag repeatedly, and became more distressed when prompted several times.

Output:

- context;
- adult language;
- child response;
- possible communication breakdowns;
- mature methods that may help;
- questions to ask a therapist or educator;
- next-step observation ideas.

Important boundary:

The AI should not say “the child must have meant X.” It should only suggest possible communication breakdowns that need professional confirmation.

## v1.0 — Integrated AI Communication Support Toolkit

Goal:

Bring the earlier modules into one coherent tool.

Possible flow:

1. Select a real-life scenario.
2. Choose a goal:
   - prepare a visual schedule;
   - simplify adult language;
   - generate communication cards;
   - document a communication event.
3. Generate materials.
4. Use in a supervised setting.
5. Record anonymous feedback.
6. Iterate based on real-world observations.

Success criteria:

- Adults can prepare support materials faster.
- Adults can communicate with shorter, clearer language.
- Children have more opportunities to express refusal, confusion, help needs, and breaks.
- Case notes become more structured.
- The project remains safe, transparent, and non-clinical.
